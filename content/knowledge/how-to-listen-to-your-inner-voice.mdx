Everyone has an inner voice. The problem is not that it stops speaking — it is that we stop listening. Here is how to hear it again.

We live in an age of noise. Notifications, obligations, other people's opinions, the constant pressure to be productive. In this environment, the quiet signal of your own inner truth gets drowned out. Not permanently. But enough that most people have forgotten what it sounds like.

Learning to hear it again is one of the most valuable things you can do.

## What the inner voice actually is

It is not a mystical concept. The inner voice is simply the part of you that knows what is true for you — what you actually want, what matters to you, what feels right and what doesn't.

It speaks in feelings more than words. A sense of excitement or dread. A pull toward something or a resistance to something else. A quiet knowing that often contradicts the louder voices of logic, expectation, and fear.

It is not always right. But it is always honest. And it is always worth listening to.

## Why we stop hearing it

Several things drown it out:

**Busyness.** When every moment is filled with tasks and stimulation, there is no space for the quiet signal to reach you.

**Fear.** Sometimes we stop listening because we are afraid of what we might hear. What if the inner voice wants something inconvenient? What if it asks us to change?

**Other people's voices.** Parents, partners, colleagues, culture — all of these carry weight. When we have been told what to want for long enough, it becomes hard to separate their voice from our own.

**Habit.** We simply get used to ignoring it. And like any ignored sense, it seems to fade.

## How to start listening again

**Create silence.** Not just absence of sound — but absence of input. Walk without your phone. Sit for ten minutes without doing anything. Let your mind wander without directing it. The inner voice often speaks in these quiet moments.

**Notice what energizes you and what drains you.** Pay close attention to how different activities, conversations, and environments affect you. The things that energize you are pointing toward something. The things that drain you are telling you something too.

**Ask yourself honest questions.** Not "what should I do?" but "what do I actually want?" Not "what is realistic?" but "what would I do if I knew I could not fail?" Write the answers down. Do not filter them.

**Pay attention to envy.** This sounds strange, but it works. When you feel envious of someone, it is usually because they have something you want for yourself. Envy is the inner voice using an uncomfortable vocabulary.

**Talk to someone who listens deeply.** Sometimes the inner voice becomes audible only when we say things out loud to another person. There is something about being truly heard that unlocks honesty in us.

## What happens when you start listening

Things become clearer. Not all at once. But gradually, you start to know what you want. You start to recognize the difference between a decision that feels right and one that is just convenient.

You start to trust yourself again.

And from that trust, a path becomes visible — one that is genuinely yours.
